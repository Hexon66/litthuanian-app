# LithuanianGo — Complete Study Plan
## From Zero to Conversational (A1 → A2 → B1)

> Designed as both a pedagogical curriculum and a development blueprint.
> Each unit = 5 lessons × 5 exercises = 25 exercises per unit.

---

## PHILOSOPHY

Lithuanian is one of the oldest living Indo-European languages. It has:
- **7 noun cases** (the biggest challenge for English speakers)
- **3 verb tenses** (past, present, future) with conjugation patterns
- **Gender system** (masculine/feminine for nouns, adjectives, pronouns)
- **Free-ish word order** but with emphasis patterns
- **Phonetic spelling** — once you learn the sounds, you can read anything

**Teaching strategy**: We follow a "spiral" approach — introduce concepts simply, then revisit them with more depth in later units. Grammar is taught through patterns, not rules.

## LEVEL 1: SURVIVAL LITHUANIAN (A1.1)
**Goal**: Say basic things, understand simple phrases, read Lithuanian text.

---

### Unit 1: First Words & Sounds
**Focus**: Alphabet, pronunciation, greetings
**Grammar**: None — pure vocabulary and phonics
**Why first**: You can't learn anything if you can't read or pronounce it.

| Lesson | Title | What You Learn |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| 1-1 | The Lithuanian Alphabet | Letters unique to Lithuanian: ą, č, ę, ė, į, š, ų, ū, ž. How they sound. |
| 1-2 | Saying Hello | Labas, Laba diena, Labas rytas, Labas vakaras, Viso gero, Iki, Ate |
| 1-3 | Polite Words | Ačiū, Prašau, Atsiprašau, Taip, Ne, Gerai |
| 1-4 | How Are You? | Kaip sekasi? Puikiai / Gerai / Neblogai / Blogai. Kaip tu? |
| 1-5 | My Name Is... | Aš esu [vardas]. Mano vardas yra... Malonu susipažinti. O tu? |

**Exercises mix**: Listening (pronunciation), Multiple Choice, Fill-in-blank
**Key audio**: Every new word gets TTS so learner hears correct pronunciation.

---

### Unit 2: Numbers, Days & Time
**Focus**: Counting, calendar, telling time
**Grammar**: None — vocabulary building
**Why here**: Numbers are essential and don't require grammar knowledge.

| Lesson | Title | What You Learn |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| 2-1 | Numbers 1-10 | vienas, du, trys, keturi, penki, šeši, septyni, aštuoni, devyni, dešimt |
| 2-2 | Numbers 11-100 | Pattern: -lika (teens), dvidešimt, trisdešimt... šimtas |
| 2-3 | Days of the Week | Pirmadienis → Sekmadienis. Šiandien yra... Vakar buvo... |
| 2-4 | Months & Seasons | Sausis → Gruodis. Pavasaris, vasara, ruduo, žiema |
| 2-5 | What Time Is It? | Kiek valandų? Valanda, minutė. Rytas, diena, vakaras, naktis |

**Exercises mix**: Matching (number ↔ word), Listening, Fill-in-blank, Sentence Arrange

---

### Unit 3: About Me
**Focus**: Basic self-description, nationality, age
**Grammar**: "Aš esu" (I am), masculine/feminine endings (-as/-is vs -a/-ė)
**Why here**: First real grammar, but kept simple — just "I am + adjective/noun."

| Lesson | Title | What You Learn |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| 3-1 | I Am From... | Aš esu iš [šalies]. Lietuvis/lietuvė. Countries in Lithuanian. |
| 3-2 | How Old Are You? | Man yra [skaičius] metų. Kiek tau metų? |
| 3-3 | My Family | Mama, tėtis, brolis, sesuo, šeima, draugas/draugė |
| 3-4 | He/She/They | Jis/Ji/Jie/Jos. Jis yra studentas. Ji yra studentė. Gender endings. |
| 3-5 | What Do You Do? | Studentas, mokytojas, gydytojas, programuotojas + feminine forms |

**Key grammar moment**: First exposure to gender. "-as/-is" = masculine, "-a/-ė" = feminine.
**Exercises mix**: Multiple Choice, Fill-in-blank (gender endings), Matching (m↔f pairs)

---

### Unit 4: Everyday Things
**Focus**: Common objects, colors, adjectives
**Grammar**: Adjective-noun agreement (didelis namas / didelė mašina)
**Why here**: Builds vocabulary while reinforcing gender agreement naturally.

| Lesson | Title | What You Learn |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| 4-1 | Colors | Raudonas, mėlynas, žalias, geltonas, baltas, juodas + agreement |
| 4-2 | At Home | Namas, kambarys, stalas, kėdė, lova, langas, durys |
| 4-3 | Food Basics | Duona, pienas, vanduo, obuolys, sūris, kava, arbata |
| 4-4 | Big & Small | Didelis/didelė, mažas/maža, gražus/graži, geras/gera |
| 4-5 | This Is... | Tai yra... Čia yra... Ar tai yra...? Questions with "Ar" |

**Exercises mix**: Matching (color + noun), Multiple Choice (agreement), Listening, Sentence Arrange

---

### Unit 5: Present Tense Verbs
**Focus**: What I do every day — present tense conjugation
**Grammar**: Present tense -a/-i/-o conjugation groups
**Why here**: Can't have conversations without verbs. This is the gateway.

| Lesson | Title | What You Learn |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| 5-1 | I Do, You Do | Aš dirbu, tu dirbi, jis/ji dirba. First conjugation (-a group). |
| 5-2 | I Speak Lithuanian | Kalbėti, mokytis, skaityti, rašyti. Second conjugation (-i group). |
| 5-3 | Daily Routine | Kelti(s), valgyti, eiti, miegoti. Reflexive verbs intro (-tis). |
| 5-4 | Likes & Wants | Noriu, galiu, turiu + infinitive. "Aš noriu valgyti." |
| 5-5 | Questions & Negation | Ar tu kalbi? Ne, aš nekalbu. Ką tu darai? |

**Key grammar moment**: Verb conjugation — the second pillar of Lithuanian.
**Pattern teaching**: Show conjugation tables visually, drill with fill-in-blank.

## LEVEL 2: GETTING AROUND (A1.2 → A2.1)
**Goal**: Handle basic real-life situations — ordering food, asking directions, shopping.

---

### Unit 7: At the Café
**Focus**: Ordering food and drinks, restaurant vocabulary
**Grammar**: Accusative case intro (Noriu kavą / I want coffee-ACC)
**Why here**: Accusative is the easiest case and has immediate practical use.

| Lesson | Title | What You Learn |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| 7-1 | The Menu | Meniu, patiekalai: sriuba, mėsa, žuvis, salotos, desertas |
| 7-2 | I Want... (Accusative) | Noriu kavą (not kava). Accusative endings for -a/-ė nouns. |
| 7-3 | How Much? | Kiek kainuoja? Eurai ir centai. Sąskaita, prašau. |
| 7-4 | Likes & Preferences | Man patinka... Aš mėgstu... Ar jums patinka...? |
| 7-5 | Full Café Dialogue | Order a meal from start to finish |

**Key grammar moment**: First case! Accusative = direct object. "Noriu kavĄ" not "kavA."
**Teaching approach**: Don't explain "cases" abstractly — just show "when you want something, the ending changes."

---

### Unit 8: Getting Around Town
**Focus**: Directions, transport, locations
**Grammar**: Locative case (Aš esu Vilniuje / I am in Vilnius)
**Why here**: "Where are you?" requires locative. Pairs naturally with places.

| Lesson | Title | What You Learn |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| 8-1 | Places in Town | Parduotuvė, bankas, ligoninė, stotis, mokykla, bažnyčia |
| 8-2 | Where Is...? | Kur yra...? Čia, ten, arti, toli, šalia, priešais |
| 8-3 | In the City (Locative) | Vilniuje, Kaune, parduotuvėje. Locative endings. |
| 8-4 | By Bus, By Car | Autobusas, traukinys, taksi. "Važiuoju autobusu" (Instrumental preview) |
| 8-5 | Direction Dialogue | Ask for and give directions in a city |

---

### Unit 9: Shopping
**Focus**: Stores, prices, clothes, transactions
**Grammar**: Genitive case intro (with numbers & quantities)
**Why here**: "Two coffees" = "dvi kavos" — genitive after numbers > 1.

| Lesson | Title | What You Learn |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| 9-1 | At the Store | Parduotuvė vocab: krepšelis, kasa, pardavėjas, pirkėjas |
| 9-2 | Clothes | Marškinėliai, kelnės, suknelė, batai, striukė + sizes |
| 9-3 | How Many? (Genitive) | Daug, mažai, keli + genitive. Du obuoliai, penkios kavos. |
| 9-4 | This One, That One | Šitas/šita, anas/ana. Demonstrative pronouns. |
| 9-5 | Buying Things | Complete shopping dialogue: find item, ask price, pay |

---

### Unit 10: Past Tense
**Focus**: Talking about what happened yesterday/last week
**Grammar**: Past tense conjugation (-au, -ai, -o / -iau, -iai, -ė)
**Why here**: A2 level requires past tense. Foundation verbs are already known.

| Lesson | Title | What You Learn |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| 10-1 | Yesterday I... | Vakar aš dirbau, valgiau, ėjau. Past tense -a group. |
| 10-2 | What Did You Do? | Ką tu darEI vakar? Tu dirbai, tu valgei. |
| 10-3 | He/She Did | Jis dirbo, ji valgė. Third person past. |
| 10-4 | I Didn't... | Aš nedirbau. Negation in past tense. |
| 10-5 | Weekend Story | Tell a story about your weekend using past tense |

---

### Unit 11: Future Tense & Plans
**Focus**: Making plans, talking about what will happen
**Grammar**: Future tense (-siu, -si, -s)
**Why here**: Completes the tense trio. Now learner can talk about past, present, future.

| Lesson | Title | What You Learn |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| 11-1 | Tomorrow I Will... | Rytoj aš dirbsiu, valgysiu, eisiu. Future endings. |
| 11-2 | Making Plans | Ką tu darysi savaitgalį? Aš keliausiu / susitiksiu su draugais. |
| 11-3 | If... Then... | Jeigu bus geras oras, eisiu pasivaikščioti. Simple conditionals. |
| 11-4 | Let's Do It! | Suggestions: Einam! Gal galėtume...? Ar nori eiti...? |
| 11-5 | My Week Plan | Describe plans for the whole week |

---

### Unit 13: Feelings & Health
**Focus**: Emotions, body parts, at the doctor
**Grammar**: Dative case (Man šalta / I am cold — lit. "to-me cold")
**Why here**: Lithuanian expresses feelings with dative, which is unique and important.

| Lesson | Title | What You Learn |

| 13-3 | I'm Cold / I'm Hot | Man šalta, man karšta, man nuobodu. Dative + adverb pattern. |
| 13-4 | At the Doctor | Sergu, turiu temperatūrą, kosulys, sloga. Doctor dialogue. |
| 13-5 | Giving Advice | Tau reikia... Tu turėtum... Impersonal constructions. |

### Unit 14: Home & Daily Life
**Focus**: Detailed daily routines, household
**Grammar**: Reflexive verbs fully (-tis: praustis, rengtis, mokytis)
**Why here**: Daily life descriptions need reflexives, which are very common in Lithuanian.

| Lesson | Title | What You Learn |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| 14-1 | Morning Routine | Keliuosi, prausiu(si), rengiuosi, pusryčiauju |
| 14-2 | My House | Virtuvė, miegamasis, vonios kambarys, svetainė + furniture |
| 14-3 | Chores | Valyti, plauti, gaminti, skalbti. "Aš gaminu vakarienę." |
| 14-4 | Weekends | Ilsiuosi, sportuoju, susitinku su draugais, žiūriu filmą |
| 14-5 | Describe Your Typical Day | Full paragraph-style description exercise |

### Unit 15: Genitive Deep Dive
**Focus**: Possession, quantities, prepositions
**Grammar**: Genitive case fully — the most-used Lithuanian case
**Why here**: Genitive appears everywhere. This dedicated unit prevents confusion.

| Lesson | Title | What You Learn |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| 15-1 | Whose Is This? | Kieno? Mamos, tėčio, draugo. Genitive = possession. |
| 15-2 | A Lot / A Little | Daug vandens, mažai laiko, kiek pinigų? |
| 15-3 | From / To / Near | Iš, nuo, iki, prie, po + genitive. Preposition patterns. |
| 15-4 | I Need... | Man reikia pinigų / laiko / pagalbos. Reikia + genitive. |
| 15-5 | Genitive Practice | Mixed exercises combining all genitive uses |

---

### Unit 16: Dative & Giving
**Focus**: Indirect objects, expressing feelings/needs
**Grammar**: Dative case fully — to/for someone
**Why here**: Dative enables expressing opinions and interpersonal actions.

| Lesson | Title | What You Learn |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| 16-1 | I Like It | Man patinka muzika. Tau patinka sportas? Patikti + dative. |
| 16-2 | Giving & Sending | Duodu draugui. Siunčiu mamai. Indirect objects. |
| 16-3 | For Me, For You | Man, tau, jam, jai. Dative pronouns. |
| 16-4 | I Need To... | Man reikia eiti. Tau reikia mokytis. Reikia + dative + infinitive. |
| 16-5 | Complex Feelings | Man atrodo... Tau atrodo...? Man įdomu, man gaila. |

---

### Unit 17: Weather & Nature

## APP FEATURES TO BUILD

### Must-Have (for this curriculum to work)

1. **Grammar Tips Panel** — Before each unit, show a short explanation card:
   - What grammar concept is coming
   - Simple table/visual showing the pattern
   - 2-3 example sentences
   - Closeable, re-viewable from lesson screen

2. **Vocabulary Bank** — Track all words learned:
   - Organized by unit
   - Searchable
   - TTS playback on each word
   - Shows: Lithuanian, English, gender, example sentence

3. **Spaced Repetition Review** — Daily review queue:
   - Words/exercises from 1 day ago, 3 days ago, 7 days ago, 30 days ago
   - Failed items come back sooner
   - Shows on home screen: "5 words to review today"

4. **Unit Intro Page** — Before starting each unit:
   - What you'll learn
   - Grammar preview (visual, not wall of text)
   - Vocabulary preview (10-15 key words with audio)
   - Estimated time

5. **Checkpoint Tests** — Units 6, 12, 18, 23:
   - Timed exercises
   - Score out of 100
   - Badge/achievement on completion
   - Must score 70%+ to unlock next level

### Nice-to-Have (future enhancements)

6. **Daily Challenge** — One random exercise from past units:
   - Bonus XP for completion
   - Streak multiplier (day 7 = 2x XP)

7. **Cultural Notes** — Short cultural facts after certain lessons:
   - Lithuanian traditions, food, places
   - Fun facts that make the language feel alive

8. **Pronunciation Mode** — Record yourself, compare with TTS:
   - Side-by-side audio comparison
   - Focuses on hard sounds: ž, š, č, ė, ų

9. **Mistake Tracker** — Track which exercises you get wrong:
   - Generate personalized review lessons
   - "Your weak areas: genitive endings, future tense"

10. **Achievement System**:
    - First Lesson, First Unit, All Units in Level 1
    - 7-day streak, 30-day streak, 100-day streak
    - Perfect lesson (no mistakes)
    - 1000 XP, 5000 XP

## GRAMMAR REFERENCE (For Curriculum Content Creation)

### Cases Quick Reference

| Case | Question | Use | Example |
|------|----------|-----|---------|
| Nominative | Kas? (Who/What?) | Subject | **Katė** miega (The cat sleeps) |
| Genitive | Ko? (Whose/Of what?) | Possession, quantities | Mamos **katės** (Mom's cat) |
| Dative | Kam? (To whom?) | Indirect object, feelings | **Man** patinka (I like) |
| Accusative | Ką? (What? — object) | Direct object | Matau **katę** (I see the cat) |
| Instrumental | Kuo? (With what?) | Means, accompaniment | Su **kate** (With the cat) |
| Locative | Kur? (Where?) | Location | **Namuose** (At home) |
| Vocative | — | Addressing someone | **Kate!** (Oh, Cat!) |

### Verb Conjugation Patterns

**Present**: dirbu, dirbi, dirba, dirbame, dirbate, dirba
**Past**: dirbau, dirbai, dirbo, dirbome, dirbote, dirbo
**Future**: dirbsiu, dirbsi, dirbs, dirbsime, dirbsite, dirbs
